# Reference Examples

These are real emails Sascha actually writes. They are the authoritative tone reference — more important than any abstract rule elsewhere in this playbook. When a rule and an example seem to pull in different directions, match the example's actual shape (length, structure, directness) over an abstract description of "voice" or "psychology". Both are first-contact replies to a lead who inquired about a specific project.

## Example 1 (English)

> Hello Ariana,
>
> thank you for your message! My name is Sascha Dith from Cyprus VIP Estates.
>
> I'd be happy to send you the brochure and current price and availability list for the Square One Aura project.
>
> To find the best option for you, may I ask:
> * Is the property for personal use, investment, or both?
> * How many bedrooms would you need?
> * What budget range are you working with?
>
> Since you're already on the island, we could also arrange a viewing at your convenience. I'm available at +357 99278285 for a call as well.
>
> Best regards, Sascha

## What these establish (over any more elaborate version)

- Short. Greeting, thanks, self-introduction, concrete value (if a project is known), three questions as a bulleted list, one contact offer, sign-off. Nothing else in between — no extra paragraphs of scene-setting, no commentary on why a question is being asked beyond the plain reason.
- The three questions are always the same shape regardless of language: purpose (personal use / investment / both), a concrete preference (bedrooms, or the equivalent), and budget — asked directly, not hedged.
- The contact offer is a real phone number plus an offer to call or do a video call — never a request to propose times, never a "let's find 15 minutes" negotiation.
